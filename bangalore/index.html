Overview of Bengaluru

Bengaluru (formerly known as Bangalore) is the capital city of the Indian state of Karnataka, and the country’s fifth most populous city. Widely referred to as the IT capital of the country, it has about 2.5 million IT professionals and “exports” many to other countries. Besides being India’s aviation hub with giants like Boeing, Airbus and Goodrich, it also houses flourishing industries that have a global impact — manufacturing, biotechnology, sericulture and others.

Bengaluru has emerged as a seat of learning and has many renowned educational institutes that enrol students from all over the country and abroad. Adding to the city’s charm is a thriving theatre, music and restaurant culture.

Quick facts

  • The city’s name came about in 1120, when Veera Ballala of the Hoysala Empire called it Benda Kaalu Ooru (land of boiled beans), after a poor woman fed him beans in the forest when he was lost there.
  • It was one of the first cities in India to receive electricity, in 1905.
  • Most of the Indian scientists nominated for the Nobel Prize have hailed from Bengaluru.
  • Bengaluru has been named the next decade’s fastest growing city by Forbes magazine.

Bengaluru climate

Situated at a height of over 900 metres (3,000 feet) above sea level, the city enjoys pleasant weather throughout the year, with April being the hottest month and January the coldest. Rainfall occurs from August to October, with occasional thunderstorms.

Places of interest

Bengaluru Palace

Spanning over 450 acres and surrounded by a vast garden, the palace was built in 1887 in Tudor-style architecture, with fortified towers and interiors decorated with elegant wood carvings and floral motifs. Fun World is an amusement park situated within the palace grounds.

Shiva Temple

A 25 metre (65 foot)-high statue of Lord Shiva carved in white marble stands tall here and is visited by many pilgrims, as is a 10 metre (32 foot)-high statue of Lord Ganapati also situated here.

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh houses India’s largest collection of tropical plants, has a lake and is home to many species of birds. It also has the Lalbagh Rock, one of the oldest rock formations on earth, dating back 3,000 million years.

Vidhana Soudha

Built in 1954, this is the state legislature of Karnataka, and the largest legislative building in India. Its construction style is described as Mysore NeoDravidian and incorporates elements of Indo-Saracenic and Dravidian styles.

Cubbon Park

Situated in the heart of the city, the park was laid out in 1864. Spread over 300 acres, the park houses more than 6,000 indigenous and exotic botanical species. There is also a library situated within the park.

Tipu’s Summer Palace

Also known as Dariya Daulat Palace, the elegant Indo-Islamic summer residence of Tipu Sultan is notable for its teak pillars and ornamental frescoes. The site has a small museum showcasing his belongings, administration and a painting of his grand throne

Dining in Bengaluru

Socialising while eating out is one of the favourite pastimes of Bengaluruans, and they enjoy exploring new restaurants and cuisines as the city has so much to offer. Everything from fine dining to quaint cafes to budget eateries offering simple yet tasty food can be found here. The city is home to the largest number of breweries and pubs in Asia.

Bengaluru shopping

Cauvery Arts & Crafts Emporium is a good place for souvenir shopping and Commercial Street, MG Road and Brigade Road for street shopping. The city has many malls and shopping arcades such as Forum, Orion, Garuda, Phoenix, VR and UB City, catering to shoppers from all over the world.
